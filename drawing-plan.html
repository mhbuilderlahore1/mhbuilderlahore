Drawing Plan

2D & 3D drawing plans, floor layouts and architectural design

Overview

Every successful construction project begins with a clear, well-considered drawing plan. No matter how skilled the builder or how good the materials, a building constructed from a poor or inadequate design plan will have problems that no amount of quality workmanship can fix — rooms that don't work in practice, spaces that are too small or awkwardly shaped, structural layouts that waste material costs, and utility plans that require expensive rework. At MH Builder Lahore, we have been producing design and drawing plans for residential, commercial, and industrial projects across Lahore since 1998, and we have learned from long experience what makes the difference between a design that works beautifully on paper and one that works beautifully in real life.

Our drawing plan service begins with a free site visit to your plot. During this visit, our design consultant measures and records the plot dimensions and shape, assesses the access points and street frontage, checks for any neighbouring structures or infrastructure that will influence the design, and reviews any society or authority layout restrictions that apply to the plot — such as setback requirements, maximum coverage ratios, height restrictions, or specific facade requirements mandated by the DHA, LDA, or other relevant authority. All of these real-world constraints are incorporated into the design brief before we begin drawing, so that the final design is practical and approvable from day one.

Two-dimensional floor plans are the foundation of any design package. Our 2D floor plans show the complete spatial organisation of every floor of the building — room sizes and shapes, door and window positions, staircase layout, kitchen and bathroom fixture placement, and utility connection points. We draw our floor plans to a scale that allows the owner to understand the spatial relationships between rooms and to verify that every space is large enough for its intended furniture and use before construction begins. We typically produce two or three layout alternatives for each project, allowing the client to compare options and select the approach that best matches their priorities — whether that means maximising the number of bedrooms, creating the largest possible living space, or achieving the most efficient room arrangement for a commercial or office building.

Three-dimensional elevation visualisations bring the design to life in a way that 2D drawings cannot. Our 3D elevations show the complete exterior appearance of the building from the front, side, and rear, including the roof design, window and door styles, exterior finish materials, boundary wall design, and entrance gate. For clients who find it difficult to visualise a building from floor plans alone — and many people do — the 3D elevation is the moment the design becomes real. We produce realistic 3D renderings that accurately represent the proposed materials and finishes, so clients can approve the exterior design with confidence before the first course of bricks is laid.

Interior 3D visualisations are available as part of our enhanced design packages, showing the proposed interior finish of key spaces — the living room, master bedroom, kitchen, and bathrooms — with accurate representation of tile patterns, cabinet designs, colour schemes, and fixture styles. These interior renders allow clients to review and adjust the interior design concept before any materials are purchased or work begins, avoiding the expensive material changes and rework that result from clients discovering mid-construction that a chosen finish does not look as expected in the actual space.

Legal compliance is a non-negotiable element of every drawing plan we produce. All our designs are checked against the applicable building regulations and society bylaws before they are submitted to the client for approval. This includes LDA or DHA building plan submission requirements, coverage ratios and setback distances, floor-to-floor height restrictions, and any specific requirements of the society or street in which the plot is located. We prepare and submit the formal building plan approval application on behalf of our clients, coordinating with the relevant authority and responding to any queries or revision requests. Many clients who approach us after starting with other designers have encountered costly delays because their earlier design did not meet the applicable regulations. Our legal compliance check prevents this problem entirely.

Structural layout drawings — showing the column and beam positions, slab thickness and reinforcement details, and foundation type and depth — are produced by our qualified structural engineer for all projects that require structural work. These drawings are submitted to the structural review as part of the building plan approval process and are used by our construction team throughout the build to ensure that every structural element is built exactly as designed.
